Finding 01Overall Happiness · 2024
Western Europe leads on overall happiness — but its score has been declining for four consecutive years.
Avg. happiness score · 2024 · Scale 0–10
Global average 2024: 5.48 · Three of four regions sit above the global average
W. Europe peaked at 7.13 in 2020 and has fallen each year since — reaching 6.97 in 2024, its lowest since 2016.
Latin America (6.33) scores higher than its wealth alone predicts — ranking 3rd on GDP but 2nd on happiness.
Middle East & N. Africa scores 1.64 points below W. Europe — the largest gap between any two adjacent regions.
Finding 02Trend 2011 → 2024
East Asia has gained more ground than any other region — +0.62 points over 13 years.
Happiness score trend · 2011–2024 · Regional averages
2013 not available · Middle East & North Africa (MENA) shown dashed
East Asia (+0.62) improved every year since 2019 and closed the gap with Latin America from 0.49 to just 0.21 points.
Middle East & N. Africa (−0.28) is the only region ending 2024 lower than it started in 2011. It hit a low of 5.11 in 2022.
Latin America (+0.34) recovered strongly after a 2020–21 dip and now sits at its highest point on record.
