Finding 01 /
Western Europe leads on overall happiness — but its score has been declining for four consecutive years.
Average happiness score by region · 2024 · Scale 0–10
Global average score in 2024: 5.48 · Three of four regions sit above the global average
W. Europe peaked at 7.13 in 2020 and has fallen in each year since — reaching 6.97 in 2024. This is the first time the region has dropped below 7.00 since 2016.
Latin America (6.33) scores higher than its wealth alone would predict. It ranks 3rd on GDP per capita among the four regions, yet 2nd on happiness — suggesting non-economic drivers are strong.
Middle East & N. Africa scores 1.64 points below W. Europe — the largest gap between any two adjacent regions. Three of four regions sit above the global average of 5.48.
Finding 02 /
East Asia has gained more ground than any other region since 2011 — a rise of +0.62 points over 13 years.
Happiness score trend · 2011–2024 · Regional averages
2013 not available in WHR · Middle East & N. Africa (MENA) shown as dashed line
East Asia (+0.62) shows the most consistent upward trajectory. It has improved in every single year since 2019, and closed the gap with Latin America from 0.49 points in 2011 to just 0.21 points in 2024.
Middle East & N. Africa (MENA) (−0.28) is the only region to end 2024 lower than it started in 2011. It reached a low of 5.11 in 2022 — reflecting regional instability — before a partial recovery to 5.33.
Latin America (+0.34) showed resilience: after a dip during 2020–21, it recovered strongly and now sits at its highest point since records began.

Finding 04 /
Middle East & N. Africa (MENA) has a higher GDP than Latin America — yet scores 1.0 point lower in happiness.
GDP score vs. Happiness score · 2024 · Bubble = relative gap from expected
Dashed diagonal = expected relationship if GDP perfectly predicted happiness · Points above line = happier than wealth predicts · Points below = less happy than wealth predicts
Latin America sits above the expected line — social cohesion and personal freedom compensate for relatively low wealth.
W. Europe also sits above the line — institutional trust and low corruption amplify wellbeing beyond what GDP alone would generate.
Middle East & N. Africa sits well below the line despite a GDP score comparable to Latin America. Freedom (0.66) is the region's lowest dimension and its largest gap vs. the leader.
East Asia sits close to the expected line — GDP and happiness are broadly aligned. Its strongest relative asset is consistent upward momentum.
